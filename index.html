Suggested Workflow
01
Start with your budget
Use the Smart Budget Estimator to understand your current monthly cash flow and project what your spending will look like in retirement. This gives you a target monthly income number to work backwards from.
02
Estimate your Social Security benefit
Run the Social Security Estimator to see how much guaranteed monthly income you can expect. Try different claiming ages to understand the trade-offs. Your full retirement age (FRA) depends on your birth year — the SSA sets this between 66 and 67 for most current workers.
03
Project your 401(k) or IRA
Take your budget target, subtract your estimated Social Security income, and use that gap as your withdrawal target in the 401(k) Projector. Model different market scenarios to understand longevity risk.
04
Consult a professional
These estimates are a starting point, not a plan. Take your results to a certified financial planner (CFP®) or CPA who can account for your full tax picture, estate considerations, healthcare costs, and more.
